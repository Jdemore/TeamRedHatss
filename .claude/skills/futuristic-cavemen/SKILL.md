# Futuristic Cavemen -- Creative Direction

Genre: **VR Rhythm Game** (Meta Quest, Unity 6000.4.0f1)
Theme: **Futuristic Cavemen** -- a world where prehistoric life collides with advanced technology.

## The Concept

Imagine cavemen who discovered technology millions of years before humanity. Stone tools glow with circuitry. Dinosaurs wear bioluminescent armor. Caves pulse with holographic cave paintings. The beat of drums echoes through neon-lit primordial forests.

The aesthetic is: **raw, primal materials (stone, bone, hide, wood) infused with glowing energy lines, holographic projections, and impossible technology.**

Think: Far Cry Primal meets Tron. Flintstones meets Cyberpunk. Monster Hunter meets Beat Saber.

## Core Pillars

### 1. Primal Energy
Everything is powered by a mysterious energy the cavemen call "いにしえ の ちから" (inishie no chikara -- "ancient power"). It flows through the earth, through dinosaurs, through rhythm itself. When you hit notes on beat, you channel this energy.

### 2. Dinosaurs Are Everywhere
Dinosaurs are not just enemies -- they are companions, instruments, obstacles, and the landscape itself. A sleeping sauropod is a mountain. A pterodactyl carries the score display. A raptor's footsteps set the beat.

### 3. Rhythm Is Magic
In this world, rhythm has physical power. Drumming the right pattern opens stone doors, calms raging dinosaurs, and charges ancient machines. The gameplay IS the lore.

### 4. Crude but Advanced
Technology exists, but it looks handmade. Circuits are etched into stone slabs. Holograms flicker like campfires. Screens are made of polished crystal. Nothing looks sleek or manufactured -- everything looks like a genius caveman built it from what was available.

## Quick Reference: Theme Elements

### Materials Palette
| Material | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Stone | Dark basalt, obsidian, sandstone -- with glowing circuit-line engravings |
| Bone | Dinosaur bones used as structural elements, polished ivory white with energy veins |
| Hide | Rough leather and fur, sometimes with bioluminescent stitching |
| Wood | Gnarled, ancient wood with glowing sap lines |
| Crystal | Transparent/translucent stones that serve as "screens" and "lenses" |
| Vines | Overgrown tech -- plants growing through and around ancient machines |

### Technology Style
- Circuits look like cave paintings -- angular, geometric, hand-drawn lines.
- Holograms are low-resolution and flicker, projected from crystal nodes.
- Energy glows in warm colors (amber, orange, magenta) or cool bioluminescent (cyan, green).
- No plastic, no metal, no glass. Everything is organic or mineral.
- Sound comes from resonating crystals, taut hides, hollow bones.

### Color Palette
| Role | Colors | Hex Examples |
|------|--------|-------------|
| Background (caves) | Deep charcoal, dark brown, midnight blue | #1a1a2e, #2d1b0e, #0f0f23 |
| Primary energy | Amber, molten orange | #ff9f1c, #e85d04 |
| Secondary energy | Cyan, bioluminescent teal | #00f5d4, #06d6a0 |
| Accent / danger | Magenta, volcanic red | #ff006e, #d00000 |
| Neutral / bone | Warm ivory, sandstone | #f2e9de, #c9b99a |

### Fonts and UI Style
- UI panels look like stone tablets or stretched hide.
- Text uses a chunky, angular font that evokes carved stone (or Hiragana for the Japanese layer).
- Buttons are bone toggles or stone switches with satisfying click sounds.
- Menu transitions: stone slabs sliding, crystals lighting up, cave paintings animating.

## Rhythm Game Integration

### Note Skins
| Note Type | Visual | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Tap note | Glowing stone orb | A floating rock with energy circuits |
| Hold note | Bone beam | An elongated dinosaur bone pulsing with light |
| Swipe note | Claw mark | Three glowing slash lines in the air |

### Hit Effects
| Judgment | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| Perfect (かんぺき) | Golden energy burst with cave-painting particles |
| Great (すごい) | Cyan crystal shatter |
| Good (いい) | Green moss puff |
| Miss (はずれ) | Dull stone crumble, dust cloud |

### "Instruments"
The player's VR controllers represent:
- **Bone clubs** -- the primary hitting tool. Thick dinosaur femurs with crystal tips.
- **Stone mallets** -- alternative skin. Obsidian heads on wooden handles.
- **Claw gauntlets** -- for swipe-heavy charts. Raptor claws mounted on hide gloves.

### Musical Style
The soundtrack blends:
- Tribal percussion (taiko, djembe, log drums).
- Electronic bass and synth (the "futuristic" layer).
- Nature sounds (dinosaur calls, thunder, flowing water) as rhythmic elements.
- Occasional chanting in pseudo-ancient language.

## Naming Convention

All in-game names should use the Hiragana skill for Japanese text. Pair every Hiragana label with romaji in code comments.

Examples:
- Song select screen title: ほらあな の おと (horaana no oto -- "Sounds of the Cave")
- Score screen: かり の けっか (kari no kekka -- "Hunt Results")
- Difficulty levels: かんたん / ふつう / むずかしい / たつじん
- Power-up: いにしえ の ちから (inishie no chikara -- "Ancient Power")
